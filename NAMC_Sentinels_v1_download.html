What The Sentinels Are

Not Volunteers. Professionals.

Every Sentinel is hired directly by the NAMC — not a volunteer, not a freelancer. A full-time league employee with a contract, a salary, and benefits. Trained and certified by the CVMA. Neutral to every team, every manufacturer, every rider.

Authority Structure

Chain of Command

The Chief Sentinel commands the combined 24-member corps and holds supreme, final authority on all on-track determinations, safety calls, and immediate race-day rulings. Chief Sentinel decisions are final and cannot be overridden mid-event. Post-event appeals are directed to the Court of Appeal.

The 24-Member Corps — 12 + 12 Model

Per rulebook 1.8, the Sentinel officiating corps is a 24-member unit: 12 Traveling Sentinels (deployed to every round for uniform enforcement across the USA, Canada, and Mexico) and 12 Local Sentinels (site-specific venue experts integrated at each round).
