The Ladder

Five Steps To The Draft

Step 1 — Beginner

Ages 4–7 · 50cc
  • Entry point for all riders. 50cc machinery. Peewee and Mini classes.
  • Focus: bike control, safety, track awareness. No OVR rating at this stage.
  • Development Series registration available from age 4. Family creates a public rider profile.

Step 2 — Junior

Ages 8–11 · 65cc / 85cc
  • Structured regional competition begins.
  • OVR rating assigned at age 9. Updates every 60 days based on regional results.
  • Compete in NAMC-affiliated regional events tracked toward the national OVR leaderboard.

Step 3 — Intermediate

Ages 12–14 · 85cc / 112cc / 150cc
  • Competition at regional and national level.
  • OVR updates every 30 days. Scouts and charter team reps begin tracking riders.
  • Top 100 OVR-ranked riders in each age class published publicly on namcmotocross.com.

Step 4 — Advanced

Ages 15–17 · 250cc / 450cc Amateur
  • Full-size amateur machinery mirroring professional race formats.
  • OVR updates every 14 days. Direct comparison against Development Series peers.
  • OVR 70+ enters the pre-draft watchlist visible to all charter teams. No pro contracts at this stage.

Step 5 — NAMC Development Series

Age 17–18 · Pre-Draft Season
  • The final step before the NAMC Draft. Official NAMC Development Series competition.
  • OVR updates every 14 days. Scores of 80+ indicate full draft eligibility.
  • Every rider publicly posted with OVR rating, home state, and class.
18-YEAR AGE FLOOR — NON-NEGOTIABLE. NO EXCEPTIONS. EVER.
The OVR Rating System

Transparent, Trackable, Public

Every Development Series rider receives a public OVR (Overall Rating) score from 1 to 100. 50 is average. 70+ enters the pre-draft watchlist. 80+ is full draft eligibility. Update frequency is tied to ladder step: every 60 days at Step 2, every 30 days at Step 3, every 14 days at Steps 4 and 5.
