## Section 12.7 — Success Ballast (Balance of Performance)

**Purpose.** Success Ballast is a per-rider Balance of Performance measure applied to preserve season-long competitive parity and curb runaway single-rider dominance. It operates within the NAMC BOP framework (see Glossary: *Balance of Power*). Ballast is carried as NAMC-certified, sealed weight affixed at a location specified by the Technical Committee.

**Accrual.** After each Main Event, a rider's carried ballast is adjusted by their finishing result in that class:

| Result | Adjustment |
|--------|-----------|
| Main Event win | **+2 kg** |
| Podium (2nd–3rd) | **+1 kg** |
| Finish outside the podium | **−1 kg** |
| DNF / DNS | no change |

**Floor & Cap.** Carried ballast never drops below **0 kg** and never exceeds **8 kg** (approximately 0.6% lap-time effect at full load on a ~110 kg class machine). Adjustments beyond either bound are truncated to the bound.

**Carry-over & Reset.** Ballast carries round-to-round within a season and **resets to 0 kg at the start of each championship season.**

**Per-Class, Per-Rider.** Ballast is tracked and applied per class (350 Pro / 250 / 250P / Women's 250) and **follows the rider**, not the charter. It does not transfer between classes.

**Certification & Enforcement.** All Success Ballast is NAMC-certified, sealed, and verified at pre-race technical inspection by the Sentinel corps. Removal, modification, relocation, or bypass is a **terminal violation** (consistent with intake-restrictor enforcement).

### Cross-references
- **Glossary — BOP (Balance of Power):** "The technical adjustment process — restrictor plates, ballast weights — used to maintain competitive parity between different engine architectures." (Success Ballast extends this framework to results-based parity.)
- **Section 8 — BOP Baselining:** engine-architecture parity (Commission-set restrictor/ballast). Distinct from Success Ballast, which is results-based.
- **Section 12 / 13 — Technical & Penalties:** terminal-violation enforcement basis for ballast tampering.

### Design notes (for the rulebook editor)
- Real-world basis: Super GT success ballast (+2 kg per championship point, 50 kg cap). Scaled here to a per-result model with an 8 kg cap for a ~110 kg motocross machine.
- Ballast **follows the rider** (talent parity in a draft-based league), not the bike/charter.
