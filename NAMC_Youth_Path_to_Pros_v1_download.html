Youth Path to Pros

It starts at age 4. A public, trackable, merit-based pipeline built for your kid — from the first 50cc gate drop to draft eligibility.

The Early Ladder

Where Every NAMC Career Begins

The youth pipeline covers the foundational steps of the NAMC Development ladder — building bike control, safety, and competition experience long before a rider is old enough for the professional Draft.

Step 1 — Beginner

Ages 4–7 · 50cc
  • Entry point for all riders. Peewee and Mini classes.
  • Focus: bike control, safety, track awareness. No OVR rating yet.
  • Development Series registration available from age 4 — family creates a public rider profile.

Step 2 — Junior

Ages 8–11 · 65cc / 85cc
  • Structured regional competition begins.
  • OVR rating assigned at age 9. Updates every 60 days.
  • Results tracked toward the national OVR leaderboard.

Step 3 — Intermediate

Ages 12–14 · 85cc / 112cc / 150cc
  • Regional and national competition.
  • OVR updates every 30 days. Scouts begin tracking riders.
  • Top 100 OVR-ranked riders per age class published on namcmotocross.com.

Steps 4 (Advanced, 15–17) and 5 (NAMC Development Series, 17–18) complete the ladder into the Draft. See the full Path to Pros development page for the complete pipeline and OVR system.

18-YEAR AGE FLOOR — NO EXCEPTIONS. EVER.

Any rider who has not reached 18 cannot enter the NAMC Draft or sign a professional contract under any circumstances. They race the Development Series until they are of age. Registration opens July 1, 2026 at namcmotocross.com.
